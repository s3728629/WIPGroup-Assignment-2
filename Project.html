What the project will do

Upon successful completion of the mobile application it will fast become the number one travel application for public transport in Victoria. By this time the application will have gone through rigorous testing to ensure it will be able to handle many users, their information and give them the best possible user experience. Ideally the application will attract a partnership with public transport operators in Victoria as it will be the best for them in regards for promoting and using their product. This could lead to further projects with possibilities to collaborate with the Victorian government, Metro/Country trains or Yarra Trams. Also, this will allow for better integration of software on the application working with payments and ticketing for public transport and other outside systems. This application will assist with journey planning incorporating disruptions, ticketing and we will test and incorporate what users want for additional features. The project will take the best parts of PTV and Myki and their competing apps with a user focus to improve on what they want and solving their problems. This could be features for the elderly to assist with not getting lost, calendar synchronisation for working professionals and friend locations for millennials.

It is assumed that the project will be programmed and built using Android Studio 4.0 as the application is planned to release on android devices initially due to their ease of access and ability to test. It also minimises the need for a software license in that regard, however if we wish to publish our application on the google play store or other android variants, we may require the use of Google play’s app licensing feature.

The hardware needed will include an android compatible device (for the first release of the application) which has Near-Field Communication hardware within it, this is just a small NFC chip that is most likely already installed in most modern smart devices.


How we will do it

We have a 12-step plan to fully explore, test, research and market the mobile application. For the scope of this project in 4 weeks, we will only be completing step 1 and exploring some parts of step 2. Daily meetings will be held after the initial 4 weeks during the working week to incorporate Agile project management to keep everything transparent and all project members informed of actions being taken and tasks completed.

Over 10 weeks afterwards, if we were going ahead, we would be completing step 2,3 and possibly near the end start on step 4 and creating a prototype.

A project of this magnitude would likely take around 6 months or longer as it’s a large project with many skills, technical knowledge and investment required. As we progress through the steps, we would need to either upskill ourselves quickly or bring in professionals who we would likely need to pay. This means that we would need to seek investment between steps 5-8. There are already other mobile transport applications so it’s important to make sure the user experience is optimal and that the product is only released to market when it will definitely be an improvement on competing applications. Otherwise it will likely not be reviewed favourably and would not create the kind of buzz and positive first impression needed to grow in the crowded mobile application marketplace.

Testing

According to our plan, the whole project process will involve three test stages, including user requirements test in the early stage of the project, black box and white box technology test in the development process, and a small range of user prototype test.

In the first four weeks of the project, we focused on user requirements testing. Because it is very important to understand the user's function in our project. What types of public transport supporting applications do they expect? Can the ticketing mode without card realize more convenient interactive experience? Do the elderly, children and special groups have equal access to our services? And whether the user's mobile phone can generally realize the digital daily public transport function and so on. We will randomly look for users taking public transport and invite them to participate in our pre prepared questionnaire. 100 users of different ages can provide us with correct and meaningful data. Once we have identified the strengths and weaknesses of the project and the level of user acceptance of the functionality provided in the application. We can move on to the next phase of prototyping and more testing.

When the project is implemented to the middle stage (when we have completed the main construction and some functions of the software), the black box test will be the first test after coding, because it is conducted from the perspective of users according to the corresponding relationship between input data and output data. It tests each function for proper operation. In the test, as a box that can't be opened, the program only checks whether the function of the program is in normal use in the test program interface regardless of the internal structure and internal characteristics under the condition of the program. According to the terms of the requirements and specifications, the program can correctly receive the input data and generate the correct output information. Black box testing focuses on the external structure of the program without considering the internal logic structure. It mainly tests the software interface and software functions. By performing this test, we do not need to understand the internal structure of the program code to fully simulate the use of the software by the end user of the software product, and check whether the software product meets the needs of the user. It can check the realization of the functional requirements of the tested system better and more truly from the perspective of users. After checking the input and output of items with black box test, you can use white box test to test the internal coding part.

White box testing is the opposite of black box testing, focusing on the code itself. Through static analysis and dynamic analysis, it points out whether the representation and description of software are consistent, conflicting or fuzzy, and analyzes the behavior of software system before, during and after the execution of software system in simulated or real environment. By checking the internal logical structure of the software, we can cover the logical path in the software. Checkpoints are set at various points of the program to check the state of the program to determine whether the actual running state is consistent with the expected state. By performing white box tests, we can optimize our software as we examine each branch and path in our code. However, it will also cost us expensive money and resources, because too many things / logic need to be checked in the code. But white box testing is a necessary way to optimize our software.

Finally, we plan to invite a small number of users for internal testing when the application is initially completed. Users use our application to ride the public transport system to evaluate the functions we provide. When we get user feedback, we optimize the application and test it again to determine whether the application reaches the goal.

Overall, doing well in these three phases of testing will be very helpful to our application. In addition, we should continue to track application reviews and improve application availability and functionality based on user needs.

Risks

The potential risk that was able to be identified in our public transport companion application is the use of our software, the ability to combine cards into our application and the possibility of users feeling that their application is similar to other apps. Due to having our application being available for android users we have chosen the software android studios to develop the app, which has created potential risks when developing the application because it has a history of being difficult to learn which can cause difficulties when trying to make an app to the best of its capabilities, furthermore has also being known to be inconsistent due to being slow and the constant need of updating makes it difficult to work with. The main risk for our public transport companion application would be ensuring that users do not view it as being similar or the same as PTV or Myki mobile services but instead something bigger and greater than those applications which users are able to view as a better and different version.

Additionally, there are also other major risks that can take place when developing the app and one of them is being able to identify the right type of personnel to effectively contribute in making the app. For example, when implementing the technology, it is important to ensure the individual can properly handle the technology being used, to ensure that the money being provided to us is not going to waste and is able to be facilitated to other areas of the mobile development, ultimately leading to a negative reputation and potentially cause less money to be invested into the public transport companion application. This could halt the development of the mobile application and can result in the app not being the best version it can be as it is not getting the optimal resources and support needed to create the application.
